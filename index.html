Ana's Loan Request

Description:

Ana is 38 years old, married, and is both a housewife and farmer. She has worked in agriculture ever since she was a young girl when her parents taught her about the land where she now grows coffee, corn, and beans. Ana has four children. The eldest attended school for nine years, the second completed primary school, and the last two are in different grades in primary school. A grandson also lives with her.

The land she and her husband work is their own thanks to a purchase from one of their customers some time ago. It is located 15 minutes away by road; sometimes she goes there by horse or donkey.

Ana is seeking a loan from the Comixmul Cooperative so she can pay for technical assistance with her crops. She would like to know more about weeding, fertilization, manure, etc., in order to get better harvests.

Her future plan is to be able to grow a better variety of coffee like Bourbon, Caturra, and Lempira so that the beans she grows will be competitive and attractive to local and national markets.

Ana's dream is to see her children attend university and get good jobs.

Loan Purpose:

Buy agricultural supplies and pay for technical assistance.

Felipe's Loan Request

Description:

Felipe is a young Tlaxcalan farmer continuing the family tradition. He and his family have grown crops and raised animals on the Tlaxcalan high plains for as long as they can remember, and for Felipe, the way of life still represents his principal source of income.

As a young innovator, Felipe is looking to take advantage of technology to improve his production. With his 10 cubic meter Sistema Biobolsa biodigester, Felipe will be able to improve his yields, reduce the need to purchase expensive and damaging fossil fuels, and reduce the environmental impact of his farm. This loan represents 46% of the total cost of his biodigester.

Loan Purpose:

Purchase a 10 cubic meter Sistema Biobolsa biodigester.

Savorn's Loan Request

Description:

Savorn, 38, is a mother with three children. Currently, she needs to support only one child who is in school. The main sources of her family’s income are rice farming (on 1.5 hectares of land) and fishing selling. These businesses generate about 19,400 KHR per day for her. Including this loan, Savorn and her co-borrower are third-time borrowers with VisionFund (Kiva's partner). This group of two is requesting a new loan of 1,000,000 KHR each. As the leader, Savorn will spend her entire loan portion to buy rice seed, fertilizer, gasoline for irrigation, and insecticide for her crop. She hopes that she can triple the yield of her crop so that she can keep her child in school until tertiary level.
